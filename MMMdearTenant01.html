If Violation of Your Rights Occurs

HUD/RAD encourages property owners to work together. If you have a problem with your unit or your living conditions, your first step is to contact the onsite property manager. If you do not receive satisfaction, you or, if you wish the Tenant Association, can file a complaint with evidence to the HUD Field Office.

The owner is then given 30 days to respond or rectify the issue. The HUD Field Office will follow up with the property owner to ensure compliance.

 

SFHA and John Stewart "Condo conversion"

From Helen Hale, Director of Housing Services, May 28, 2024

"I did notice one very important misstatement. It is true that the RAD owners have a 99-year lease for these properties. But they are built on land owned by SFHA which requires that they remain low income (or Affordable Housing). Thus, owners would renew their lease or SFHA and the City (as we are partners) would find another affordable housing owner to take over ownership and operations of the property. No RAD owner could convert them to market rate housing without the federal government approving that plan (which they wouldn’t as they are in the business of providing affordable housing or low-income housing). The City has regulations in place to make it difficult to sell any low-income properties to convert them to market rate buildings. Basically the City has first right of refusal which means we have the opportunity to buy the building in order to find another affordable housing owner/operator. So, your buildings are protected by their agreement with SFHA and with the City. We have no desire to lose any low-income buildings to market rate owners."
